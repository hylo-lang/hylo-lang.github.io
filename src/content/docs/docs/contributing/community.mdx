## Ways to Be Part of Hylo

- **Curious Visitors**<br/>
  Explore our [documentation](/docs), join discussions on <a href={discussionsLink}>GitHub</a>/<a href={slackLink}>Slack</a>, and follow our progress on our [blog](/blog).
- **Contributors**<br/>
  Help improve Hylo by reporting issues, submitting pull requests, or propose new research topics. Get started with our [contributing guide](/docs/contributing/) or just let us know on Slack (ping `@Ambrus Toth`), and we can arrange an onboarding meeting. You are also welcome to join our regular developer meetings.
- **Maintainers**<br/>
  When you have a deep understanding of one of Hylo's subsystems, you can become a maintainer to review contributions, and guide or lead development.
- **Core Team**<br/>
  If you are deeply committed to Hylo's vision and actively contribute in discussions and development activities, you will be recognized as being part of the core team.
- **Researchers**<br/>
  If you write a paper related to Hylo or answering some of our open research questions, we would love to hear about it and feature it on our website. We are also open to research collaborations, hosting university projects and try to help along the way.

## Values and Culture

1. **Curiosity and Research**<br/>
   We push the frontier on all aspects, including language design and tooling, by being research-driven and studying the industry's existing approaches
   and relevant academic literature, engaging with domain experts of our problems, and experimenting with new ideas.
   We are building the core language on strong theoretical foundations to have a system that is sound and scalable for the long term.

2. **Collaboration over Competition**<br/>
   We see language development as a shared effort, not a zero-sum game. We learn from Swift, Rust, C++, and
   Odin among others, and we give back through blog posts, bug reports, patches, research findings, and libraries we build along the way. By sharing
   our work early and helping others, we get feedback, connections, and make faster progress than we would alone. We value the
   world's success first, the community's second, and personal success third - generosity ultimately strengthens all
   of them.

3. **Evolving Practical Trade-offs**<br/>
   We don't believe a successful systems programming language can exist without trade-offs, nor that we can get them all
   right from the start. Therefore, we aim to document our design decisions in <a href={discussionsLink}>GitHub Discussions</a>, so that we can revisit
   them later and make it easy to add resources and comments on them over time.
