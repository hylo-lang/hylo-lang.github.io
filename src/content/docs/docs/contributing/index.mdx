---
title: Contributing to Hylo
---

Developing a new language is a challenging, but fun undertaking. We are happily welcoming individuals who are interested
in contributing to Hylo, getting started in compiler development or any other aspect of the project. You can always
introduce yourself on Slack, ask questions or guidance for getting started. We are also open to [research collaborations](/docs/contributing/research-collab) and
hosting university projects.

Here are some ideas for ways to contribute:
- reporting issues
- participating in [Discussions](https://github.com/orgs/hylo-lang/discussions)
- compiler development
- devops
- improve our documentation and website
- writing blog articles related to Hylo or about relevant research
- tooling development (vscode extension, language server, debugging support, etc.)
- research in tooling, compiler technology, or in language design - see [Research Collaboration](/docs/contributing/research-collab)

To get an overview of the major repositories, see [Infrastructure Overview](/docs/reference/infra). You can look at the
open issues of each repository you are interested in, where you can usually find issues labelled as **good first issue**.
